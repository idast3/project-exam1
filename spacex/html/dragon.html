BROUGHT THE GOODS

Dragon wrapped up SpaceX's 16th resupply mission to the International Space Station as it landed in the Pacific Ocean on Sunday, January 13, packed with about two tons of cargo and science.

Filled with more than 5,600 pounds of supplies and payloads, Dragon launched aboard a Falcon 9 rocket on December 5, 2018 from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station in Florida. It arrived at the space station on December 8.

The Dragon spacecraft flown on this mission previously visited the space station during SpaceX’s CRS-10 mission in February 2017.

DID YOU KNOW?

Dragon is the only spacecraft currently flying that is capable of returning significant amounts of cargo to Earth.
